About
I am an Assistant Professor of Korean Linguistics at Dongguk University. My passion for the Korean language began early in life and led me to pursue a career in studying and teaching Korean linguistics. I have long been interested in how language reflects thought, culture, and human interaction. Through the study of Korean, I seek to better understand Korean language and culture, and ultimately gain deeper insights into human communication and understanding.
Research
My research lies at the intersection of Korean linguistics, formal semantics, discourse pragmatics, and artificial intelligence. I am particularly interested in how linguistic meaning is constructed and interpreted in Korean, focusing on syntax–semantics interfaces, modality, tense and aspect, discourse interpretation, and expressive meaning. A central goal of my research is to investigate Korean language understanding from both theoretical and empirical perspectives. My recent work explores emotional meaning, discourse, and temporal interpretation in Korean. More recently, I have become interested in AI-based approaches to Korean linguistics, particularly how large language models (LLMs) understand, generate, and structure Korean language.
Teaching & Being Taught
I love being a teacher. At the same time, I often feel that teaching is also a process of being taught. My students—both undergraduate and graduate—continually challenge me to rethink familiar questions and explore Korean language in new ways. Their curiosity and thoughtful questions often push me to think more deeply about language, meaning, and communication than I would on my own.

My journey into Korean linguistics began with a deep love for the Korean language. As a student at Sogang University, I had the privilege of learning syntax from Professor Chung-Hoon Lee, whose classes taught me not only how language is structured, but also how careful and rigorous thinking can deepen our understanding of human language. His encouragement to build strong foundations regardless of circumstances has remained an important principle throughout my academic life.

Books
발견을 위한 한국어 의미론
Korean Semantics for Discovery
명정희 지음 · 서강대학교출판부, 2025.3.27. · 2025 세종도서 학술 선정도서
이 책은 여러 형식적 기호를 나열하기보다는, 우리가 사용하는 언어의 의미를 바라보는 형식 의미론의 핵심적인 관점을 제시하고 함께 경험하는 데 중점을 둔다. 이런 맥락에서 국어학을 학술적으로 연구하는 전문가뿐만 아니라 언어 자체에 관심이 있는 이들이라면 누구나 이해할 수 있도록 논의를 진행하고자 한다. 그러기 위하여 형식 의미론의 전문 용어 자체보다는, 그러한 용어가 필요한 이유와 원리에 초점을 맞출 것이다. 그러면 독자들은 전문 용어보다 흥미로운 언어 현상 자체에 주목할 수 있을 것이다. 이를 통해 한국어가 보여주는 여러 의미 현상이 어떻게 체계적으로 설명될 수 있는지 발견하는 즐거움을 함께 누리기 바란다.
Rather than enumerating formal symbols, this book focuses on presenting the core perspective of formal semantics — the study of how meaning works in the language we use — and invites readers to experience it firsthand. With this in mind, the discussion is written to be accessible not only to specialists in Korean linguistics, but to anyone with a genuine interest in language itself. To that end, the emphasis is placed not on the technical terminology of formal semantics per se, but on the reasons and principles that make such terminology necessary. This allows readers to attend to the fascinating linguistic phenomena themselves, rather than getting lost in jargon. Through this approach, the book hopes to share the joy of discovering how the diverse semantic phenomena of Korean can be explained in a systematic way.
